# Cross-Portfolio Evidence Index

Reviewed 2026-07-19. Use this as an index, not as a substitute for the source notes and caveats inside each idea dossier.

## Municipal evidence

- [NYC 311 Service Requests from 2020 to Present](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Social-Services/311-Service-Requests-from-2020-to-Present/erm2-nwe9) - structured, current public service-request records suitable for a bounded read-only prototype.
- [US EPA: Extreme Heat](https://www.epa.gov/climatechange-science/extreme-heat) - describes extreme heat as the leading weather-related cause of death in the United States.
- [NYC Heat Vulnerability Index](https://a816-dohbesp.nyc.gov/IndicatorPublic/data-features/hvi/) - neighborhood-level framework showing that heat risk is distributed unequally.
- [US Census Bureau: Renter Cost Burden](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2024/renter-households-cost-burdened-race.html) - nearly half of renter households were cost-burdened in 2023.
- [NYC HPD Complaints and Problems](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Housing-Development/Housing-Maintenance-Code-Complaints-and-Problems/ygpa-z7cr) and [Violations](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Housing-Development/Housing-Maintenance-Code-Violations/wvxf-dwi5) - public condition reports and verified code violations.
- [NHTSA: Pedestrian Safety](https://www.nhtsa.gov/road-safety/pedestrian-safety) - reports a pedestrian killed every 74 minutes in U.S. traffic crashes in 2024.
- [NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Public-Safety/Motor-Vehicle-Collisions-Crashes/h9gi-nx95) and [School Point Locations](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Education/School-Point-Locations/jfju-ynrr) - inputs for a school-zone proximity prototype.
- [US EPA: Lead Service Lines](https://www.epa.gov/ground-water-and-drinking-water/lead-service-lines) and [NYC Lead Service Line Coordinates](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Environment/Lead-Service-Line-Location-Coordinates/jqfp-uff7) - national context and local public inventory data.
- [ADA.gov: Title II Web and Mobile Accessibility Rule](https://www.ada.gov/resources/2024-03-08-web-rule/) - WCAG 2.1 AA requirements and compliance timelines for covered state and local government content.
- [NYC Comptroller: Late Contracts Dashboard](https://comptroller.nyc.gov/services/for-the-public/late-contracts-dashboard/) and [Caught in the Slow Lane](https://comptroller.nyc.gov/reports/nyc-contracts/) - public timeliness evidence and vendor impact analysis.
- [NYC Capital Projects Dashboard](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/City-Government/Capital-Projects-Dashboard-Citywide-Budget-and-Spe/gyhf-rsr3) - public capital budget, spend, history, and variance data.

## Public-education evidence

- [IES: Chronic Absenteeism](https://nces.ed.gov/use-work/supporting-recovery-with-evidence-based-practices/chronic-absenteeism) - more than 14 million students were chronically absent in 2021-22 and missed at least 10 percent of school days.
- [NCES: Students Behind Grade Level](https://ies.ed.gov/learn/press-release/about-third-public-school-students-ended-2023-24-school-year-behind-grade-level-least-one-academic) - school leaders estimated 32 percent ended 2023-24 behind grade level in at least one subject.
- [The Nation's Report Card: 2024 Reading](https://www.nationsreportcard.gov/reports/mathematics/2024/g4_8/supporting-files/summary-of-results.pdf) - reported continued grade 4 and grade 8 reading declines.
- [CDC: Adolescent Mental Health](https://www.cdc.gov/healthy-youth/mental-health/index.html) - 2023 national high-school mental-health indicators.
- [NCES: Limits on School Mental-Health Services](https://nces.ed.gov/learn/press-release/over-half-public-schools-report-staffing-and-funding-limit-their-efforts-effectively-provide-mental) - staffing, funding, and licensed-provider access barriers.
- [NCES: Students With Disabilities](https://nces.ed.gov/programs/coe/indicator/cgg/students-with-disabilities) - 7.5 million students ages 3-21 served under IDEA in 2022-23, or 15 percent of public-school students.
- [NCES: English Learners](https://nces.ed.gov/programs/coe/indicator/cgf/english-learners) - 5.3 million public-school students, 10.6 percent, were English learners in fall 2021.
- [NCES: 2024-25 Hiring Challenges](https://nces.ed.gov/learn/press-release/most-u-s-public-elementary-and-secondary-schools-faced-hiring-challenges-start-2024-25-academic-year) - vacancy, certification, and hiring-difficulty indicators.
- [NCES: School Traffic Safety](https://nces.ed.gov/learn/press-release/more-one-third-public-schools-agreed-traffic-patterns-around-schools-pose-threat-student-safety) - 38 percent of school leaders agreed traffic patterns posed a physical-safety threat while commuting.
- [Federal Student Aid: FAFSA Completion by High School](https://studentaid.gov/data-center/student/application-volume/fafsa-completion-high-school?nt=1) - official aggregate completion data for current application cycles.

## Interpretation rules

1. National and citywide statistics establish importance, not the local prevalence or cause of a problem.
2. Public datasets can contain errors, lag, missingness, and changing field definitions. Show source dates and preserve caveats.
3. A hackathon prototype is not evidence of effectiveness. Establish a baseline and pilot with users before claiming an outcome.
4. Recheck every URL, deadline, dataset schema, and program rule immediately before a public presentation.
5. For education, health, accessibility, procurement, and safety workflows, obtain domain and legal review before using real records.
